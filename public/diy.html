How To Repair A Zipper


A simple and easy to follow guide. Suitable for most fabrics including cotton, denim, polyester and linen.

Materials Needed

  • Replacement Zipper
  • Matching Colour Thread
  • Seam Ripper
  • Zipperfoot [optional]

Video Guide

Instructions

Find a Replacement Zipper

  1. Measure existing zipper.
  2. Buy a zipper of matching colour and length.

Remove the Previous Zipper

  1. Take note of how the old zipper is positioned.
  2. Using a seam ripper to cut through the zipper stitches.
  3. Gently pull on the zipper once stitches have been removed.
  4. Remove stray threads.

Attach the New Zipper

  1. Position new zipper.
  2. [Optional] Install a ZipperFoot.
  3. Thread your sewing machine with the matching thread colour.
  4. Lower the presser foot.
  5. Sew a straight stitch along the edges.
  6. Trim off any loose threads.

How To Repair A Fallen Hem


A simple and easy to follow guide to re-hem a garment.

Materials Needed

  • Needle
  • Matching Colour Thread
  • Scissors
  • Pins/Marker

Video Guide

Instructions

Fold The Hem

  1. Iron the garment to remove creases.
  2. Measure your hemline while wearing the garment to ensure the correct height. Mark the point with pins or chalk to test.
  3. If there is excess fabric, trim it to 2cm below the new hemline.
  4. Fold the hem up.

Sew The Hem Using A Whipstitch

    Hide your thread knot and bring the thread from the inside through the outside of the hem fold. Moving from right to left (or left to right if you're left-handed), cross over diagonally and pick up a few threads of the fabric above the fold. Keep the needle pointed in the direction in which you're working. Bring the needle back out through the fold and repeat.

Stitch The New Hem

  1. Measure and cut your thread, you need more thread than the circumference of the hem.
  2. Thread your needle and tie a knot at the other end. Flip your garment inside out.
  3. Start with a tiny stitch at the seam line on the wrong side of the hem.
  4. Stitch around the hemline using the above whipstitch.

  6. Try on the garment to test the the wrong hemline.

How To Repair A Hole, Rip, or Tear


A simple and easy to follow guide to repair a hole, rip, or tear in a garment.

Materials Needed

  • Needle
  • Matching Colour Thread

Video Guide

Instructions

Thread The Needle

  1. Use a thick needle for thick fabrics or a thin needle for soft fabrics.
  2. Cut the thread approximately 2.5 times the length of the hole or rip.
  3. If there is excess fabric, trim it to 2cm below the new hemline.
  4. Thread the needle and tie a knot at the end.

Fix The Hole/Rip/Tear

  1. Turn the garment inside out, and cut away any loose threads.
  2. Pinch the edges of one end together so that they're aligned on the inside.
  3. Approxiumately 2mm from the edge, insert the needle and pull all the way through.
  4. Thread the needle back through edge 2mm along and continue this every 2mm.
  5. When you get to the end, loop the needle through the final stitch to tie off the thread.
  6. Once you have done this a few times, snip off the thread just above the knot.

How To Replace a Button


A simple and easy to follow guide to replace the button on a garment.

Materials Needed

  • Original Button or Similar Replacement Button
  • Matching Colour Thread

Video Guide

Instructions

Setting Up

  1. Thread the needle so there is an equal length on both sides and tie a knot in the end.
  2. Position the button so it lines up with the previous button stitching or the other garment buttons.
  3. Check that the button lines up with the button hole.

Replacing/Reattaching the Button

  1. Push the threaded needle up through the fabric and one hole of the button and pull through.
  2. Place a pin between the button and the fabric to prevent it from being stitiched too tight.
  3. Push the needle down through the opposite hole and repeat twice.
  4. From the underside, switch to the other set and repeat twice.
  5. Remove the pin and loop the thread between the button and the material six times.
  6. Push the needle back to the underside of the fabric.
  7. Make three or four stitches to secure the thread and cut off the excess.
